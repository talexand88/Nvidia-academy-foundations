Lists

Bulleted and numbered lists for narrative content. Up to three nesting levels, small NVIDIA-green bullets, sentence case throughout.

Source. The Academy list style is set by the canonical PowerPoint reference: a 3-level hierarchy at 44 / 40 / 36 pt, small green dot bullets, with the option to mix bullet lists and un-bulleted paragraphs in the same block.

Three List Shapes

Unordered (bullets)
  • Each item starts with a noun or verb
  • Sentence case throughout
  • No trailing periods on short items
Ordered (steps)
  1. Start the container
  2. Mount the volume
  3. Run the script

Rules

Sentence case. First letter capitalized, rest lowercase. Acronyms and proper nouns keep their capitalization.

No trailing periods on short items. If items are full sentences, end them with periods. If they're fragments ("Mount the volume"), no periods.

Maximum three levels of nesting. If you need a fourth level, the slide is doing too much — split it into two slides.

One verb per item in step lists. Don't combine actions ("Mount the volume and configure the network" → split into two steps).

Do

  • Single-clause items
  • Same grammatical form
  • One verb each
Do
Parallel structure
Items mirror each other in length and grammar — easier to scan.

Mix bullets with paragraphs

Don't

  • Long winding item that runs to the next line and keeps going for a while
  • Different grammar
  • Also has a verb and a noun and then a clause
Don't
Mismatched item lengths and grammar
