Notifications

A white card with a colored left stripe. Carries a title, a body, and an optional next-step action. Use when the message needs more weight than a Banner.

Source. Sourced from the Kaizen UI Foundations Notification component. Four statuses: Informational, Success, Warning, Error.

When To Use Which

StatusUse case
InformationalProvides additional information that may require action.
SuccessConfirms to the user that they have completed an action or task.
WarningCautions the user on something urgent to avoid an issue.
ErrorCommunicates a critical issue that needs attention and provides a next step.

Banner Vs. Notification — Which One?

BannerNotification
LengthOne lineTitle + 1–2 line body
ActionNoneOptional next-step button
BackgroundTinted with status colorWhite, with status stripe on the left
Use forPage-level setup ("this lesson assumes…")Per-event messages, especially ones the learner can act on

Anatomy & Spec

Inside The Card

  • Stripe — 6 px wide, full card height, on the left.
  • Icon — 22 × 22, status-colored, next to the stripe.
  • Title — Bold, 14 px, status-neutral text color.
  • Body — Regular, 13 px, 1–2 lines max.
  • Action button — Optional, outlined, right-aligned. One per notification.

Sizing & Spacing

  • Card border — 1 px solid gray-200.
  • Radius — 6 px.
  • Padding — 14 px vertical, 18 px horizontal (inside the stripe).
  • Icon → text gap — 14 px.
  • Min height — 72 px.

In PowerPoint & After Effects

Translate the recipe. Build the notification as a horizontal group: a 6 pt × full-height rectangle filled with the stripe color, a white rectangle next to it with a 1 pt --gray-200 stroke, the icon (NVIDIA library — info / warning / error / check) at ~32 pt, a bold title at 22 pt, and a body at 18 pt. If you need an action, use the standard NVIDIA Sans Medium 18 pt outlined button.

Do

Bold title, short body
One sentence explaining the situation. That's it.
Do
Title + body + one action
Title is the headline. Body adds context. Action is the next step.

Don't

Two buttons in one notification
Both feel important.
Don't
Multiple actions
One action per notification. If the user has a real choice, that belongs in a modal, not a notification.
FYI
A long body that explains the title in three sentences. Then keeps explaining for another two. And finally adds a paragraph about the broader context which really should be in the actual page content, not in a notification card, because at this point we've stopped being a notification and become a paragraph with a green sidebar.
Don't
Paragraphs in the body
Body is 1–2 lines. Move long explanations to the main content.
Tinted background
This is a Banner, not a Notification.
Don't
Color the card body
Notification cards stay white. Only the left stripe and the icon are colored. If you want a tinted block, use a Banner.
