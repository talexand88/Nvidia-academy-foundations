Topologies

How to draw network, system, and infrastructure diagrams. Sourced from Unit 01 (icon set) and Unit 10 (Leaf-Spine, Fat-tree, Dragonfly+, Torus 3D).

The Anchor Sample — InfiniBand Fat-tree

InfiniBand
A three-tier fat-tree: spine (top), leaf (middle), hosts (bottom). Container framed in cyan with the fabric name in NVIDIA Green at the top-left. Switches use a bidirectional-arrow glyph; hosts use a rack-slot glyph. Connections are thin neutral lines that sit behind the nodes.

The Four Canonical Topologies

From Unit 10. Pick the one that matches your fabric type and follow its layout rules.

Leaf-Spine

Two rows. Leaf switches (bottom) connect down to hosts; spine switches (top) interconnect the leaves. Every leaf connects to every spine. For larger fabrics, add a third "Super Spine / Core" row above.

Fat-tree

Hierarchical tree where links near the top are "fatter" (higher bandwidth). Show by drawing the top tier at 2 pt stroke and the bottom tier at 1 pt. Visually communicates the bandwidth asymmetry.

Dragonfly+

Groups of leaf-spine sub-fabrics, connected to each other in a full mesh. Inter-group lines in NVIDIA Green, intra-group lines in neutral gray. Requires adaptive routing.

Torus 3D

3D grid with wrap-around connections in three dimensions. NVIDIA Green for X rings, Garnet for Y, CPU Blue for Z. Switches drawn as isometric cubes when scale allows. Coordinate labels at the corners.

Sizing. 56 × 36 pt for switches in a small topology (≤ 12 nodes), scale down to 40 × 26 pt for dense layouts (24+ nodes). Server racks scale taller, never wider — keep the slot-stripe pattern visible. Don't introduce new icons; if a needed node type isn't here, file a comment and we'll add it.

Connection Styles

Lines do two things: show structure (every diagram) and distinguish dimensions or roles (multi-color diagrams like Torus 3D and Dragonfly+).

Shape rules

  • Straight for hierarchical layouts — Leaf-Spine, Fat-tree, Dragonfly+ bipartite groups.
  • Curved for wrap-around connections — Torus rings, where the line has to bend back to the opposite side.
  • Crossings overlap — don't draw line "hops". Cleaner to let lines visually cross. If too many crossings make it unreadable, the diagram is too dense; split it.
  • No arrowheads on physical fabric links — connections are bidirectional by definition. Reserve arrowheads for routing flows.
  • Bundle notation — when one line represents N parallel links, label the line "×N" in small gray text rather than drawing N lines.

Framing & Labels

When the diagram represents a named subsystem — a fabric, a cluster, a pod — bracket it in a 2 pt cyan frame and put the name in NVIDIA Green at the top-left inside the frame. Conceptual / illustrative diagrams skip the frame.

Frame

  • Stroke — 2 pt, #6BB8C7 (sample cyan).
  • Fill — white (#FFFFFF).
  • Padding — 28 pt around the topology graphics.
  • Title position — top-left, inset 14 pt from the frame edges.
  • Title color — NVIDIA Green, Bold 16 pt.
  • Multiple frames — adjacent (not nested). For pod-of-pods, render each pod as its own framed unit with a short cyan connector between them.

Labels inside the diagram

  • Switch names — short: SW-A, SW-B, SW-C. Not full inventory IDs.
  • Role labels — off to the side: "Leaf / Edge", "Spine / Core", "Super Spine / Core".
  • Host labels — generic "Host / End Node" unless the role matters.
  • SM badge — small "SM" tag on the switch hosting the Subnet Manager. One per fabric.
  • 3D coordinates — +x / −x, +y / −y, +z / −z at the diagram corners (Torus and other 3D layouts).
  • Link callouts — "Internal Links" vs "External Links" pointed at representative line bundles with thin gray leader lines.
  • Group brackets — curly braces or vertical bars to enclose a tier of nodes when the tier needs a name.

In PowerPoint & After Effects

PowerPoint

  • Vector only — build with PowerPoint shapes, not pasted bitmaps. Switches scale; raster icons don't.
  • Node text — NVIDIA Sans Bold 18 pt for switch names (SW-A), 16 pt for role labels (Leaf / Edge), 14 pt for host labels.
  • Lines — 1.2 pt stroke, no glow, no shadow. Send connections to back so they sit behind nodes.
  • Coordinate labels (3D) — 14 pt, NVIDIA Sans Medium, positioned just outside the diagram frame on the matching axis.
  • Canvas — leave a 1-in margin from the master title to the top edge of the topology frame.

After Effects

  • Animate by tier — spines fade in first (220 ms), then leaves (180 ms, 80 ms after spines), then hosts (160 ms, 80 ms after leaves), then connection lines draw on (320 ms total, staggered 20 ms per pair).
  • Hold the resolved diagram for at least 2 seconds before transitioning out so the viewer can read it.
  • Highlight a route by re-coloring its lines NVIDIA Green at full opacity while dimming the rest to 30%.
  • Don't rotate 3D topologies during the explanation — keep one camera angle fixed. Rotation belongs on a separate "spin" beat, not while a label is being read.
