Banners

A full-width tinted strip that conveys a single, page-level message. No actions, no dismissal — just a short statement that sets context.

Source. Sourced from the Kaizen UI Foundations Banner component. Four semantic statuses: info, warning, error, success.

The Four Statuses

Information banner: provides neutral information to users
Warning banner: alerts users to a potentially unwanted outcome
Error banner: indicates a problem that needs attention
Success banner: confirms a successful action or operation

Anatomy & Spec

Inside The Banner

  • Icon — 20 × 20, status-colored, leftmost element.
  • Text — single line, sentence case, no period at the end.
  • No buttons, no dismiss — if the user must act, use a Notification instead.

Sizing & Spacing

  • Border — 1 px solid, color from the table above.
  • Radius — 6 px.
  • Padding — 13 px vertical, 18 px horizontal.
  • Icon → text gap — 12 px.
  • Width — full container width.

In PowerPoint & After Effects

Translate the recipe. Use a 1 pt rectangle filled with the background hex, then a darker-colored stroke from the table. Stroke roundness ~6 pt. Add the icon (from the NVIDIA icon library — pick the matching status: info/warning/error/check) at 24 pt, then the text at 18 pt next to it. Don't approximate the tint with PowerPoint's preset color picker — use the exact hex.

Pick the right status
Warning for "could go wrong", error for "did go wrong", success for "completed", info for everything else.

Don't

Title in a banner
A whole second sentence explaining the title. And maybe a third one for good measure.
Don't
Multi-line, with a title
Use a Notification when you need a title plus body. Banners are single-line.
