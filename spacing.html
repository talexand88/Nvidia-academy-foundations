Spacing & layout

Generous breathing room. Predictable rhythm. Don't crowd the edges.

Typography Color Frames & cards Spacing & layout Logos Code & CLI Charts Patterns Topologies

Slide Margins — Keep Content Away From The Edge

Standard slide margin
PowerPoint (40 × 22.5 in)80 pt
Video (1920 × 1080)96 px
All four sides. Content lives inside this safe zone. Title, body, frames, footer — nothing touches the canvas edge.
Tight (full-bleed exceptions)
Margin40 pt
For full-bleed hero compositions (course title cards, section openers). The hero image extends to the edge; the title text still respects the standard margin.
Loose (focal-content slides)
Margin120 pt
Hook-question slides, single-focal-point slides where the message benefits from extra whitespace.

Vertical Rhythm — Space Between Blocks

Title to subtitle
PowerPoint8 pt
After Effects8 px
Subtitle sits tight under its title — they read as a pair.
Title to body
PowerPoint24 pt
After Effects24 px
After a title block, before the body content begins.
Paragraph break
PowerPoint16 pt
After Effects16 px
Between two paragraphs in the same block of body copy.
Section break
PowerPoint56 pt
After Effects56 px
Between two distinct content sections on the same slide. Optional thin rule (1 pt gray-100) inside the gap.

Spacing Scale

Use values from this scale only. Don't free-pick spacing — the rhythm holds the whole composition together.
StepPowerPointAfter EffectsWhen to use
XS4 pt4 pxTight spacing inside a pill or badge
S8 pt8 pxTitle to subtitle, label to value
M16 pt16 pxParagraph break, between small grouped items
L24 pt24 pxDefault. Title to body, between unrelated cards
XL40 pt40 pxBetween card groups (Lecture row above Lab row)
2XL56 pt56 pxSection break on a slide
3XL80 pt96 pxSlide margin (canvas edge to content)

Video Safe Zone

Action safe — 6% inset
Title safe — 12% inset
Keep titles and key visuals inside the safe zone
Title safe~96 px inset · 12%
Action safe~48 px inset · 6%
Frame1920 × 1080
Title text and primary content live inside the title-safe rectangle. Background, ambient motion, and decorative elements may extend to the action-safe rectangle but never past it. Standard broadcast and projector cutoff convention.
